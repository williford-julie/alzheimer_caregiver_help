Alzheimer's Disease. What is it?

Alzheimer's disease is an irreversible brain disorder that progressively affects cognitive functionin such as thinking, remembering, and daily function activities. First termed in 1906 by Dr. Alois Alzheimer after examining the brain tissue of a women who had symptoms of memory loss. He found clumps of amyloid plaques and tangled bundes of fibers (tau tangles). We now know these are some of the features of Alzheimers disease.


Alzheimer's Disease. Signs and Symptoms

Sometimes the changes affecting the brain may actually happen a decade brfore signs and symptoms of Alzheimer's may appear. Symptoms may vary from person to person, but the most common sympton is memory loss.Forgetting to take medication, important dates, and repeating questions are common with memory loss. Problem solving challenges may also present itself with Alzheimer's Disease. Following a recipe may become more difficult. Confusion is often seen with Alzheimer's Disease. Losing track of time, date, and seasons can often by a sign of the disease. Some may notice mood changes with Alzheimer's Disease. Suspicion, aggitation, fear, and anxiety can often been seen with someone dealing with Alzheimer's.
